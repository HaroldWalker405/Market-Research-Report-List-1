<p><p>The wheat malt market is divided primarily into white malt and red malt. White malt, typically lighter in color, is characterized by its mild flavor and high enzymatic activity, making it ideal for brewing and baking. It provides a clean, crisp taste and is often used in lagers and pale ales. In contrast, red malt has a richer color and a more pronounced flavor profile, contributing deeper, toasted notes to products. It is commonly utilized in darker ales and specialty breads, adding complexity to the final product.</p></p>
