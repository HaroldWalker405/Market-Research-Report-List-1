<p><strong>The Bio-Aviation Fuel Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>FT SPK</li><li>HEFA SPK</li><li>SIP</li><li>FT-SPK/A</li><li>ATJ-SPK</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bio-aviation fuel market comprises several types, each derived from different feedstocks and processes. FT-SPK (Fischer-Tropsch Synthetic Paraffinic Kerosene) uses gas-to-liquid technology; HEFA SPK (Hydroprocessed Esters and Fatty Acids) converts oils and fats through hydrotreatment; SIP (Synthetic Iso-paraffins) is made from fermentable sugars; FT-SPK/A combines Fischer-Tropsch with conventional kerosene; and ATJ-SPK (Alcohol-to-Jet) transforms alcohols into jet fuel. "Others" encompass additional innovative pathways. These technologies aim to reduce aviation's carbon footprint by providing sustainable fuel alternatives.</p></p>
