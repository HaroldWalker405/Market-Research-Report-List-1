<p><p>The multimetal dielectric nanocomposites market is categorized into high-k and low-k types based on their dielectric constants. High-k multimetal dielectric nanocomposites feature a high dielectric constant, enhancing capacitance and enabling compact electronic devices. They are often utilized in advanced capacitors and transistors. In contrast, low-k multimetal dielectric nanocomposites exhibit a low dielectric constant, which minimizes signal delay and power loss in integrated circuits. Both types play crucial roles in improving the performance of electronic components across various applications.</p></p>
