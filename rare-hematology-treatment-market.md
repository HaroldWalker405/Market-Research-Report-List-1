<p><ul><li>Plasma Derived Factors</li><li>Recombinant Factors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The rare hematology treatment market is primarily categorized into two types: plasma-derived factors and recombinant factors. Plasma-derived factors are therapeutic products extracted from human plasma, used to treat bleeding disorders like hemophilia by providing essential clotting proteins. In contrast, recombinant factors are genetically engineered proteins that replicate the function of natural clotting factors, offering a safer, more controlled alternative. Both types aim to improve patient outcomes in rare hematological conditions by managing bleeding episodes effectively.</p></p>
