<p><p>The non-invasive Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) market includes several key technologies: EEG (electroencephalography) measures electrical activity through scalp electrodes; fMRI (functional magnetic resonance imaging) tracks brain activity via blood flow changes; NIRS (near-infrared spectroscopy) monitors cerebral oxygen levels using light; MEG (magnetoencephalography) captures magnetic fields from neural activity; and ECOG (electrocorticography) involves placing electrodes on the brain surface, although it is semi-invasive. Each modality offers unique benefits for applications in healthcare, gaming, and neurorehabilitation.</p></p>
