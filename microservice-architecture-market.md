<p><p>Microservice Architecture is a software development approach that structures applications as a collection of loosely coupled services. Each service is self-contained, performs a specific function, and can be developed, deployed, and scaled independently. This architecture enhances agility, enabling teams to innovate rapidly and adapt to changing market demands. It supports continuous integration and delivery while facilitating the use of various technologies and programming languages.</p><p>The Microservice Architecture Market is witnessing substantial growth, largely driven by the increasing demand for scalable and flexible applications among enterprises. Organizations are transitioning from monolithic systems to microservices to improve operational efficiency and accelerate time-to-market for new features. This shift is further supported by the adoption of cloud computing and DevOps practices.</p><p>Latest trends indicate a surge in the implementation of containers and orchestration tools like Kubernetes, enabling seamless management of microservices.
