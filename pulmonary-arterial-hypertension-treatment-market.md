<p><p>The Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension (PAH) treatment market includes various therapeutic classes. Vasodilators relax blood vessels to lower pressure. Phosphodiesterase 5 (PDE 5) Inhibitors enhance nitric oxide’s effects, improving blood flow. Endothelin Receptor Antagonists (ERAs) block endothelin, a potent vasoconstrictor, to reduce vascular resistance. Soluble Guanylate Cyclase (SGC) Stimulators enhance the nitric oxide signaling pathway, leading to vasodilation. Together, these treatments aim to improve patient outcomes and quality of life by managing PAH symptoms and progression.</p></p>
