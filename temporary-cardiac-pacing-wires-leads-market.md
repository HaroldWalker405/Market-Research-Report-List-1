<p><p>Temporary cardiac pacing wires and leads are crucial for managing arrhythmias and providing temporary cardiac support. The market is primarily divided into unipolar and bipolar types. Unipolar leads utilize a single electrode, allowing for a simpler design and easier implantation, but may have limitations in noise interference. Bipolar leads, on the other hand, feature two closely spaced electrodes, reducing interference and enhancing signal quality. Both types serve specific clinical needs, influencing their selection based on patient requirements and procedural contexts.</p></p>
