<p><ul><li>Autologous Bone Marrow Transplant</li><li>Allogeneic Bone marrow Transplant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bone marrow transplant market primarily comprises two types: autologous and allogeneic transplants. In autologous bone marrow transplants, a patient's own stem cells are harvested, processed, and reintroduced after intensive treatment, minimizing rejection risk. Conversely, allogeneic bone marrow transplants involve transferring stem cells from a compatible donor, which can enhance treatment efficacy but carries a higher risk of complications such as graft-versus-host disease. Both types cater to various blood disorders, including leukemia and lymphoma, driving market demand.</p></p>
